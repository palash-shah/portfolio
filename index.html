Quantitative researcher building the future of event‑driven markets.

I'm Palash Shah, a student at Carnegie Mellon University working at the intersection of mathematical finance, machine learning, and prediction market design.

My current paradigm focuses on Option-Implied Event Contracts — a complete derivatives framework that treats prediction market prices as bounded stochastic underlyings via the Jacobi diffusion. This enables options, variance swaps, and Greeks-based hedging on event futures for the first time.

Alongside, I research implied volatility surfaces through spectral clustering, LSTM forecasting, and reinforcement learning for regime-adaptive trading strategies.
